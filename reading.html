My Reviews!

Erik Larson's The Splendid and The Vile is an account of Winston Churchill and his inner circle from the resignation of Neville Chamberlin to the bombing of Pearl Harbour and the turn of the war. The book is narratively driven but well documented and without fabrication. Journal entries from the people around Churchill fill the book with perspective. This includes, among others, his private secretaries, his daughter, and various American emissaries.
In this way, Larson brings to life the people around Churchill, which in turn, provides a unique, proverbial 'fly-on-the-wall' insight. The reader feels Churchill's passion and personality whilst sympathising with the people that supported - in different ways - his struggle and eventual triumph. The book doesn't miss the ordinary. It still covers the turmoil and the impact of the blitz on the people of Britain. Entries from mass observationist, add to the story and join the dots of the war.
Fundamentally, the book paints a great portrait of Churchill's strength of character, childlike playfulness, humility and leadership. The reader is compelled to seek out further reading on the splendid and the vile of the second world war.
